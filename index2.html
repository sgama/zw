Background

Gert is a German-born half-Russian, half-German. Growing up he travelled and moved often with his family, primarily in the Eurasian part of the world. The Zwingenberger family is a bilingual household, where Gert and his sister grew up speaking & learning German and Russian as a native language.

At the German School of Moscow Gert received his middle school education and moved to Kazakhstan for his High School years at the Almaty International School. Throughout his High School years Gert became more and more interested in technical fields, taking almost every Advanced Placement course that his school offered.

In 2012 Gert was accepted into the University of British Columbia (UBC) into the Applied Sciences Program. In the Autumn of 2013, Gert started studying his main area of interest: Mining & Mineral Processing Engineering; after his first year, Gert's curiosity and interest in the Commerce and Economics fields lead him to take various courses in those fields at the University. In his third year, Gert completed the Commerce Minor Program and graduated from UBC in May, 2016 with a B.A.Sc. in Mining Engineering.

After graduating from University, Gert joined Mining Plus in the Vancouver office as a Mining Intern. As an intern, Gert worked on various international mining projects. The focus was on underground mine development and stope design with various mining software (Deswik CAD, Surpac), as well as mine estimations and PEA studies. In 2017, Gert became an Engineer-In-Training (EIT) and an official member of the Association of Professional Engineers and Geoscientists of British Columbia (APEGBC). Gert continues to work at Mining Plus and looks forward to obtaining hands-on experience at a mine site.
